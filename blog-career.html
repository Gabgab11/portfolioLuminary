Starting and growing a career in web design

Nobody starts out knowing what a "career in web design" actually looks like day to day. Most of us start by making something — a page for a friend's small business, a redesign of a site we thought looked dated — and only later realize there's a whole discipline behind what felt like instinct.

Start with real projects, not just practice files

A polished personal project is worth less than a rough one built for an actual person with actual constraints. Real clients bring real problems: content that doesn't fit the layout, brand colors that clash, deadlines that force trade-offs. Those problems teach faster than any tutorial.

Learn the tools, but lead with judgment

Framer, Figma, Webflow — the tools will keep changing. What doesn't change is the underlying judgment: knowing what to leave out, when a design serves the user versus the ego of the designer, and how to read a brand's world well enough to design something that fits it.

Build a body of work you can point to

Three or four projects you can speak about in depth beat twelve you can only summarize. Be able to explain the decisions, not just show the outcome.

Four years in, the thing that's mattered most isn't any single skill — it's staying curious about the businesses and cultures behind each brief, and letting that curiosity shape the work.
