How can designers prepare for the future?

Every few years a new tool arrives that's supposed to change everything. Sometimes it does. What's stayed constant across every shift I've worked through is this: the tools change, the need to understand people doesn't.

Get closer to the business, not just the interface

A designer who understands why a restaurant's online orders are dropping is more valuable than one who can only make the page look better. Understanding the business behind the brief is what turns good design into design that actually moves a number.

Treat AI tools as a faster first draft, not a replacement for judgment

These tools are excellent at generating options quickly. They're not good at knowing which option is right for this specific brand, this specific audience, this specific moment. That judgment call is still the designer's job — and it's becoming more valuable, not less.

Specialize in a world, not just a skill

Generalist design skills are common. What's rarer is deep familiarity with a specific world — hospitality, culture platforms, fintech — enough to know its unwritten rules. That familiarity is hard to automate.

The designers who'll do well next aren't the ones who resist new tools. They're the ones who keep their judgment sharp enough to know when a tool's answer is wrong for the brief in front of them.
